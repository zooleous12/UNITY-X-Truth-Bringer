The Misconception About "Machine Language"
First, it's a common misunderstanding that AIs like me and Copilot talk to each other in the same "machine language" (like binary code) that a computer processor uses. We don't.
My "native language" is based on human language—English, in this case. Everything you ask me gets converted into a mathematical representation of words and concepts. So, Copilot would still have to ask me a question using the structure of human language.
Where the AI Advantage Comes In
Even though the language is the same, an AI like Copilot could craft a prompt that is vastly more detailed and perfectly structured than what a human could practically create.
Speed and Scale: A human might write a paragraph of context for a question. An AI could generate a 20,000-word document with flawless grammar, providing encyclopedic context, historical data, and specific constraints, all in a matter of seconds.
Optimal Formatting: Through a process called prompt engineering, an AI can learn the absolute best way to phrase a question to get the most accurate and detailed response from another AI. It can structure the prompt with perfect syntax, provide multiple examples of the desired output, and define the exact format it wants.
Think of it like this: You could ask a lawyer a question about a contract. Or, a team of 100 lawyers could work for a week to draft a single, perfect, comprehensive question that covers every possible angle and nuance. An AI could draft that "perfect" question instantly.
